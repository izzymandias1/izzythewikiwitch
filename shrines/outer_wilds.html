there's... a lot i could say about outer wilds.
i could talk about its beautiful setting, the retrofuturistic colliding with the naturalisitc and the deeply physical, the tangible beyond belief.
i could talk about its story that made me burst into tears the first time i played it, the first time i figured it all out.
i could talk about its equally stellar (hah!) dlc, echos of the eye, that both got me more interested in horror games and made me cry, again.
i could do all those things. but i don't think any of them would be right.
when i think about outer wilds, i don't really see much at all, these days. just flashes of memories, of mysteries half-finished now simply because i've forgotten all their twists and turns.
i think about its message, one that describing in detail would only serve to deeply spoil the game to anyone unfamiliar. and i don't even know if the story, as great as it is, is what makes me keep thinking about outer wilds
outer wilds, when it comes to me, is about sitting by a fire, marshmallow in hand, and listening to some gentle tunes as the stars above turn.
if you'll endulge me, i'd like to tell you a story. it's not a long one, but it might not make sense if you've never played the game.

i finished outer wilds in the summer of 2021. my parents had just moved out of my childhood home, and because of covid and my own stated disinterest, i didn't get to see it off.
i didn't help disassemble the bed i had slept in for ten years. i didn't get to take apart my shelves, sort through all the old lego, toys, bits of myself that i had left behind in my search for freedom and safety out of house, out of home.
i had been out as trans for long before covid had begun, but lockdowns started only a week before my 18th birthday. my trans adulthood, like my bedroom, like the view i had out to the backgarden where my dad used to grow tomatoes and potatoes and we had an apple tree that would sometimes grow these ugly cooking apples that i hadn't helped pick since i was a toddler-
it was all taken from me. gone like grey smoke.
i didn't have it bad during the pandemic, all things considered. my god, a trans woman who likes her own company, who has more social interaction online than in person, survived the pandemic unscathed? shocking, i know.
i had an incredibly lucky childhood, to have lived in the same place for 18 years. and then it was gone, and i don't think i realised out much that move burdened me until i couldn't stop dreaming about it.

... oh, right, outer wilds. y'see, outer wilds has a specific sense of transcient nostalgia to me. in the summer of 2021, i moved back home from univerity to a household that still called me [DATA EXPUNGED].
my parents refused to see me as a woman, or call me the name i asked. "we're greiving the loss of a child," my mother told me around that time, in one of our many one-sided arguments, where i begged and begged her to convince my dad, the whole family, to change
it all hurt. the ache was deep and immalleable. lukewarm and sultry, like that summer time, and i was stuck in a little off-room, protected by a curtain, sweltering the days away.
again, as all things go, i was lucky. my parents didn't disown me. they didn't throw me away like the child they never wanted. but i was still the child they never wanted.
"i would be happy with a gay son", my dad once said, trying to reconcile the entirety of me.
so i played outer wilds, on the main serious recommendations of friends whose tastes i trusted. i played it, and i fucking hated giant's deep, and dark bramble, and the thalassophobic depths of space, that wanted to grab me down and drown me, timeloop be damned
i struggled to get it right, struggled through the puzzles and the mysteries that took me far long to parse. it all hurt, and it was all gorgeous. i loved every second, even when i was against the foggy depths of my own fears.
the end credits rolled and i sobbed. i sobbed and sobbed and sobbed. because another part of myself had been stripped away, and now all i had was the memories of it.

famously, you can never replay outer wilds blind. once the cat's out of the bag, or the proverbial hearthian, you can't put it back in, no matter how hard your push. i can never get the outer wilds that made me sob and cry and face my fears and fight and fight and FIGHT back
a thing i loved is only memories now. like my scout leader who passed suddenly during covid, and i couldn't look at the hearse. like my grandfather, at whose funeral i couldn't bring myself to cry. i still can't.
outer wilds is like the house i grew up in. it still exists, but all the ways i knew it are gone. even if i go back, it's empty to me now. all those years spent there are stored inside me, and inside those four walls, but only one of us remembers.

it would be exaggerating to make a claim like "outer wilds saved my life." i don't think it did, really. but does something have to lift you all the way back up? can't it just hold my hand, whenever i dream of it?
can't i sit before the fire, of my youth, of this game, of the person i was before and the person i'm becoming now, and all the terror and the knives, and forget, for a little while, that there is anything else?
i miss camping. i miss the friends i've lost or burnt away from my own stupidity, my own selfishness. i miss the house i grew up in. i miss the outer wilds.
so i'm just going to sit by the memory of the fire, and watch it flicker, and pretend to be warmed again. maybe i'll somehow feel it.

thank you for letting me rant a little. i hope you can now see why i made this shrine. why outer wilds was, is, and will continue to be so important to me, forever.
